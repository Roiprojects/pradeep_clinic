Service FAQs

Common questions about child healthcare and consultation.

When should I consult for my child's fever? expand_more
It's best to consult if the fever persists for more than 48 hours, is accompanied by lethargy, or if you're concerned about your child's overall activity and hydration levels. Early diagnosis helps in preventing complications.
How often are preventive check-ups recommended? expand_more
For growing children, an annual preventive check-up is recommended. For infants and toddlers, follow-ups are more frequent to track developmental milestones and growth markers meticulously.
Do you provide specialized nutritional guidance? expand_more
Yes, our dedicated "Nutrition Desk" offers specialized dietary advice tailored to your child's age, growth needs, and any existing medical sensitivities.
